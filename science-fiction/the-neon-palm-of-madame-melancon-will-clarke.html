From the author of Lord Vishnu's Love Handles comes the first novel ever marketed for the sole amusement of the billionaire class. While perhaps the most expensive e-book ever published, The Neon Palm of Madame Melancon is only a fraction of the cost of a presidential weekend at Mar-Lago, a Birkin handbag, or even a first class pee-pee party in Moscow. For mere plutocrat peanuts, you can now read an e-book written for the minds of Sergi Brin, Ivanka Trump, Mark Zuckerberg, and that guy who owns Chick-fil-A. 

You see, Ivanka and Chick-Fil-A guy, this sci-fi adventure was designed specifically for people like you, people who demand luxury, people who have, up until now, been forced to download regular non-luxury e-books onto their gold-plated Kindles. Our exquisite little novel fixes all that—a luxury e-book for people who want to "suck out all the marrow of life."

To those of lesser financial means, we do realize that spending this kind of money on a novel may seem extravagant, maybe even frivolous—what with the water in Flint, Michigan and those kids in Syria. But one day, we may come out with a regular person edition for people like yourselves (think Tesla in five years).

However, today our focus is squarely on acquiring a top 1% readership. At Middle Finger Press, we are striving to break the barriers of price, literacy, and even decency with our Billionaire Book Club 1st editions. In fact, this exclusive e-book is specifically targeted to the minds bold enough and the hearts brave enough to put dents into universes. 

Billionaires! 

For you people, you crazy ones, you square pegs in round holes, truly are the only ones who can change our future when it comes to climate change.

You are our only hope.

The robots are coming, boys and girls, our antibiotics are failing, and it’s getting hotter every day. So let this science fiction novel be the first place to spark your desire for a brighter future. We need your help to save this small blue planet, billionaires. We need your imagination and your dreams, and most of all your empathy. And that surprisingly enough can start with reading small works of fiction like this one. That's the thought anyway.
