“The Anthem Companion to Thorstein Veblen,” edited by Sidney Plotkin, is a stimulating, multi-disciplinary collection of new and original essays by leading scholars in the field of Veblen studies. Contributors – drawing from sociology, economics, anthropology, history and political science – explore a wide range of Veblen’s intellectual pursuits and aspects of his biography. The books includes critical and alternative examinations of Veblen’s methodology; the basis of his instinct theory; considerations on religion and moral value as well as reflections on both Veblen’s own higher education and a critical analysis of “The Higher Learning in America.” It also tackles applications of Veblen’s theory to studies of post-colonial capitalism, development of the Turkish leisure class and the implications of his work for assessments of contemporary anti-centralist political movements in the West.
