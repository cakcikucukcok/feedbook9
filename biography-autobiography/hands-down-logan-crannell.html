I began writing this book while incarcerated in an Idaho jail, facing a prison term.  I kept a journal as a form of therapy, as I came to terms with my experiences.  As my thoughts and notes developed, I noticed themes cropping up; the struggle of co-dependency, the abuse of power, synchronicity and belief systems falling apart.  I was writing extensively about boundary lines being crossed; moral betrayals, the places between waking and dream states, and the borders of life and death.  I realized that within those notepads, I hadn't made a diary – I'd produced a memoir that could potentially help others, especially those traveling dangerous roads.

At personal risk, I chose to publish it.
