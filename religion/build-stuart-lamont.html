In the book Build, author Stuart Lamont guides the reader through the story of Nehemiah with a unique prophetic edge. The book likens the city of Jerusalem to the individual believer, and the city wall to the maturity of the believer. Stuart provides readers with a practical guide to take stock of their spiritual life, and then upgrade their spiritual life in a way they may not have thought possible—by increasing their spiritual maturity and resilience. By exhorting the reader to get the basics of faith right—no matter how boring this may seem—Stuart equips the reader to be themselves, walk with God, and live a life punctuated by genuine transformation that is anything but...boring.

Readers of Build will:

  • Understand why some of the methods they use in order to grow tend to fail.
  • Become more objective about the suffering and challenges in their own life, and start building the momentum that comes from good choices.
  • Learn how to maintain their momentum of personal growth long-term, by casting off the baggage of guilt, condemnation and confusion, and to be established immovable in Christ in the midst of trials and good times alike.

Because the book will:

  • Connect with the reader through original illustrations from the author's own life.
  • Present four robust principles that the reader can start applying immediately, and thereby build momentum towards long-term change.
  • Exalt Christ as the ultimate foundation for the reader's life, and challenge the reader to put aside superficiality, perceived security and comfort, in order to take hold of the gifts that God freely offers to His children.
